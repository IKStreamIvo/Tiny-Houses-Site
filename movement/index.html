What is the Tiny House Movement?

You’ve probably heard a lot about tiny houses and its movement, but what is it exactly? The tiny house movement is basically a big group of people who choose to shrink their living space. The difference in size between a regular house and a tiny house is substantially; a typical American house is around 2500 square feet and a tiny house ranges from 100 to 500 square feet, that’s about 2000 in difference!

However, the Tiny Life isn’t only about tiny houses. Tiny Living is mainly about life simplification. It’s about doing what you want to do, in a space that gives access to your goals. Living in a tiny house also gives a lot of freedom in what you do. You don’t have to do a lot of chores, no mortgages and you’ll probably have a lot of financial sustainability.

Reasons to Live Tiny

The most important reasons people start living the Tiny Life are the financial and environmental concerns they have, but there are also smaller things to consider.

  1. Learning to let go
    Living in these small spaces forces you to live with the bare minimum, which can be very liberating because you own less, and are less dependant on items.
  2. Pursuing your passions
    Since you don’t have many bills to pay and don’t need much money, it means you will have a lot of spare time and can finally do what you want, whenever you want.
  3. Learning to let go
    Living in these small spaces forces you to live with the bare minimum, which can be very liberating because you own less, and are less dependant on items.
  4. Low-maintenance
    A tiny house is mainly self-sufficient, and there is only a small area to keep clean. This gives you the opportunity to travel to another place at any time you want.
  5. Simplicity
    (In America) The Tiny House Movement is cited as a small-scale rebellion against a culture of materialism, to push back against excessiveness.

The Legality of Tiny Living

In most areas it is illegal to live the Tiny Life. This is mainly because of living with a less financial burden. Governments want you to pay mortgages and taxes, but you’re trying to avoid them. Not every city is completely against it, however. Some allow you to live tiny, as long as you stay rooted at your spot, so that they can control the utilities and force people to pay the taxes anyway.

There are places which are specifically open to tiny houses. These has been created so that the normal cities and towns would not get overrun with them, as safety matters. A last thing that might make your house illegal, are the way they can be built. Since they’re usually portable, small, and don’t always have the same certifications as a traditional housing, they create a mess when it comes to zoning rules and building codes.
